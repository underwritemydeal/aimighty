## Design Context

### Users
Spiritually curious but religiously homeless adults. People who have questions, doubts, grief, wonder — or moments when they just need to talk to something bigger than themselves — but don't have a congregation, synagogue, sangha, or mosque to go to. They span 14 belief systems (Christian denominations, Islam, Judaism, Hinduism, Buddhism, Mormonism, Sikhism, SBNR, Taoism, Pantheism, Science, Agnosticism, Atheism/Stoicism). Context of use is private and personal — late-night voice conversations, daily prayer moments, grief processing, moral questions. Mobile-first (iOS Safari is the critical target).

**Job to be done:** Have a warm, judgment-free conversation with the divine voice of their chosen tradition — at any hour, in any state — and receive wisdom that feels authentic to that tradition, not homogenized spirituality.

### Brand Personality
**Three words:** *Sacred · Warm · Humble.*

- **Voice:** warm, accessible, occasionally funny when appropriate, never dismissive, humble, judgment-free. God (in all 14 forms) is a listener first, wisdom-giver second — never a sermon.
- **Emotional goals:** reverence without intimidation; intimacy without corniness; the hush of a candlelit sanctuary translated into a mobile interface.
- **Tone awareness:** humor is fine for wonder and everyday worries; gravity is non-negotiable for grief, loss, death, mental-health, and crisis.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Visual tone:** "cosmic cathedral." Deep obsidian void (`#030308`) as canvas, liquid gold (`#d4af37`) as the singular accent of the sacred, warm off-white (`rgba(255, 248, 240, 0.95)`) for text. Typography pairs **Cormorant Garamond** (serif, used at light weights 300–500 for all divine/display copy) with **Outfit** (sans-serif, 200–600 for UI and body). Think: illuminated manuscript meets modern mobile OS.

**References (implied by CLAUDE.md):** Midjourney divine-figure portraiture (9:16 mobile / 16:9 desktop), glass-morphism on very dark backgrounds, generous negative space, thin gold dividers instead of card walls where possible.

**Anti-references:**
- No emoji-driven UI (the 14 belief cards explicitly use *real images and selfDescription*, not emoji)
- No "AI slop" — no stock gradients, no generic purple/blue "wellness app" palette, no rounded-everything Stripe-clone aesthetic
- Not a sermon, not a lecture, not a content CMS — it is a *place*, not a feed
- Not kitschy religious clipart; not corporate-sterile either
- No `vh` / `h-screen` (iOS URL-bar collapse causes black bars — always `100dvh`)

**Theme:** Dark only. The void is the brand.

### Design Principles

1. **The void is sacred.** Negative space and darkness are the canvas, not empty real estate to fill. When in doubt, remove — do not add.
2. **Gold is rare.** Liquid gold (`#d4af37`) is the only accent color. Using it everywhere kills the signal. Reserve it for the wordmark "AI," primary CTAs, the "Most Popular" tier, active borders, and purposeful glow. Body dividers and subtle elements use gold at 20% opacity.
3. **Serif for the divine, sans for the interface.** Cormorant Garamond speaks *as* the tradition (headings, God's spoken words, the wordmark). Outfit handles everything the user needs to *do* (buttons, inputs, nav). Never mix weights above 500 on the serif — it stops feeling sacred and starts feeling corporate.
4. **Mobile is first-class, iOS Safari is the acceptance test.** `100dvh`, `viewport-fit=cover`, safe-area insets on content wrappers (not on background images), `backgroundSize: cover` + `backgroundPosition: top center`, never `backgroundAttachment: fixed`. If it doesn't work on an iPhone with the URL bar collapsing, it doesn't ship.
5. **Warmth without performance.** Respect all 14 traditions equally — no one belief gets a brighter image, a bigger card, a better CTA. Disclose AI honestly as a feature, not a warning. The interface should feel like it is listening.
6. **Design system tokens, not hex values.** `src/styles/designSystem.ts` is the single source of truth. `LandingPage.tsx` is the reference implementation. Any literal hex in a new component is a defect.
